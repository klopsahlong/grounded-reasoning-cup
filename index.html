Databricks is hosting a live competition at the Data + AI Summit 2026, where student teams will build AI agents for enterprise reasoning tasks. We invite teams to apply below—full details on the competition and prizes are included.

Overview

The Grounded Reasoning Cup is a live competition bringing together leading AI labs and top academic teams to tackle real-world enterprise challenges using AI agents.

The competition is built around OfficeQA, a benchmark developed by Databricks that evaluates how well AI systems can retrieve and reason over large collections of real-world unstructured documents in enterprise settings. Teams will have 2 months prior to the competition to build out their solutions, and will apply their agents to a new, unseen test set in the competition—testing how well solutions generalize.

This is a unique opportunity to:

  • Tackle high-impact, real-world enterprise problems with AI
  • Showcase your work in front of top researchers, engineers, and industry leaders
  • Compete for recognition—and $100k in model credit prizes—at one of the largest data & AI conferences

Good luck, and we look forward to seeing your applications!

Who Should Apply

We are looking for teams of 2–4 members to compete and attend in person. All team members must be from the same university. The development team may be larger (e.g., an entire academic lab), but the team must choose 2–4 representatives to attend in person. A travel stipend will be provided for representing team members.

We expect many teams to include PhD students, but we also encourage undergraduate and master’s students with strong research experience to apply.

Competition Format & Rules

Development Period

The main development period is April 15 – June 15. During this time, you will develop and refine your approach with the existing OfficeQA benchmark serving as a development set.

Live Competition

At the live event, teams will be evaluated on a previously unseen benchmark built from data similar to the U.S. Treasury Bulletins (the original corpus used for OfficeQA).

Competition Flow

The competition consists of 6 rounds over approximately two hours.

  • Rounds: In each round, 10 new questions from the benchmark are released simultaneously to all teams. Teams have 15 minutes to submit answers to all 10 questions.
  • Systems: You may use AI agents, automated pipelines, or human-in-the-loop review.
  • Re-Submissions: Each team has 3 re-submit opportunities throughout the competition to resubmit an answer in the current round.

Scoring

  • Correct Answer: Each correct answer earns 1 point.
  • Speed Bonus: The first team to correctly answer a given question receives a +0.25 pt speed bonus.
  • Final Ranking: Determined by total points accumulated.
